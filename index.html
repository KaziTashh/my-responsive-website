Where to start

Featured Destinations

Three landmarks worth building a full day around, each just a short drive from the Old City.

01 · Old City

Charminar

The four-minaret icon that gave Hyderabad its skyline in 1591, still standing at the center of the Old City's daily rhythm — traders, prayer calls, and bangle shops all within sight of its arches.

Hours 9:30 AM–5:30 PM Entry ₹25 / ₹300 Best for Sunset & night lighting
02 · Ibrahim Bagh

Golconda Fort

An 800-year-old hilltop citadel once at the center of the world's diamond trade. Its famous acoustics carry a hand clap at the entrance clean to the summit pavilion, nearly a kilometre uphill.

Hours 9:00 AM–5:30 PM Entry ₹15 / ₹200 Best for Evening light & sound show
03 · Hayathnagar

Ramoji Film City

The world's largest film studio complex, and a full day out in its own right — recreated streets and palace facades sit alongside live stunt shows, gardens, and an eco park built for families.

Hours 9:00 AM–5:30 PM Entry From ₹1,500 Best for Full-day family outings

Before you go

Travel Tips

A few practical notes that make the difference between a rushed visit and a good one.

☀

Best Time to Visit

October to March keeps daytime temperatures in the 15–28°C range — comfortable for the fort climb at Golconda and long walks in the Old City. April to June runs hot and dry.

🚌

Getting Around

Hyderabad Metro covers Charminar, Salar Jung Museum, and the Birla Mandir area well. Auto-rickshaws and ride-hailing apps fill the rest, including the longer hop out to Ramoji Film City.

🍴

What to Eat

Hyderabadi biryani is non-negotiable, ideally from an Old City spot near Charminar. Add haleem if you're visiting during Ramzan, and Irani chai with Osmania biscuits any time of year.

👤

Dress & Temple Etiquette

Cover shoulders and knees at Birla Mandir and the city's mosques, and expect to remove footwear before entering. Photography is restricted inside Birla Mandir.

🛒

Shopping in the Old City

Laad Bazaar beside Charminar is the place for bangles, pearls, and bridal wear. Prices are rarely fixed at the smaller stalls, so a bit of friendly bargaining is expected.

💳

Language & Currency

Telugu and Urdu are widely spoken alongside Hindi and English. Carry small denomination cash — several monument ticket counters are still cash-only or card-only in ways that vary by site.
